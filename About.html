About Me

Pull up a chair.

I'm James. I live and work in the San Francisco Bay Area. I’m currently employed as an Environmental Economist, a title and field which covers a range of related areas, like environmental planning, hazard mitigation, regulatory compliance, project risk analysis, and geospatial analysis and modeling. I spend most of my time doing benefit-cost analyses for hazard mitigation projects, contributing to regulatory documents required under NEPA and CEQA, performing spatial analyses and creating maps in GIS. What I really enjoy about this field is that the projects and results are tangible, connected to places, people, and the natural environment.

I’ve also recently started two master’s programs, in pursuit of an M.S. in GIS Development from the University of Wisconsin Madison, and an M.B.A. from CSU Stanislaus. I’m hoping to leverage this education to pursue more specialized work in developing tools for natural hazard risk modeling, mitigation alternative evaluation, and risk communication. You’ll find a few of my recent school projects on the Portfolio page. More to come soon!

My interest in the outdoors and the tangible carries over into my personal life. I’m a wilderness backpacker, car camper, rudimentary camping gear seamstress (seamster?), very amateur woodworker, and IPA enthusiast. But the hobbies don’t get much attention right, as my second full time job is our new son!

Thanks for stopping by!
-James
